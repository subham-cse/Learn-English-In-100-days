# Day 04 : Prepositions

## ðŸ“š Overview

Prepositions are essential connecting words that show relationships between nouns/pronouns and other words in a sentence. They indicate position, direction, time, manner, and other relationships.

## ðŸ” Definition

A preposition is a word that typically comes before a noun, pronoun, or noun phrase to show its relationship to another word in the sentence.


> Basic Structure: Preposition + Noun/Pronoun (called the "object of the preposition")

> Example: The book is on the table.

    "on" = preposition

    "the table" = object of the preposition


## ðŸ“Š Types of Prepositions

### 1. Prepositions of Place/Location

> Indicate where something is located

> Examples: in, on, at, under, above, below, beside, between, among, behind, in front of

    Sentences:

        The cat is under the table.

        She lives in New York.

        Put the book on the shelf.


### 2. Prepositions of Time

Indicate when something happens

> Examples: at, on, in, during, for, since, until, by, before, after

    Sentences:

        We meet at 5 PM.

        My birthday is on Monday.

        I'll see you in an hour.


### 3. Prepositions of Direction/Movement

Show movement from one place to another

> Examples: to, from, into, out of, toward, through, across, over, around

    Sentences:

        She walked to the store.

        The ball rolled under the car.

        He jumped over the fence.


### 4. Prepositions of Manner

Describe how something is done

> Examples: by, with, like, without, in

    Sentences:

        She travels by train.

        He wrote it with a pen.

        They fought like animals.


### 5. Prepositions of Agency/Instrument

Show who/what performs an action or what instrument is used

> Examples: by, with

    Sentences:

        The book was written by Hemingway.

        She opened the door with a key.


### 6. Prepositions of Purpose/Reason

Explain why something happens

> Examples: for, because of, due to

    Sentences:

        This tool is for cutting.

        The game was canceled due to rain.


## ðŸ“ Common Preposition Rules and Usage

> In, On, At (The "Big Three")

|Preposition|	Time|	Place|
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
|IN|	Months, years, seasons, centuries, long periods	|Countries, cities, enclosed spaces|
|ON|	Days, dates, specific days	|Surfaces, streets, public transport|
|AT|	Specific times, holidays	|Specific addresses, points, events|


### Examples:

    Time: I'll see you in June on Monday at 3 PM.

    Place: She's in the office on the third floor at her desk.

> By vs. Until

    By = deadline, no later than

        Finish this by Friday.

    Until = continuing up to a point

        I'll work until 6 PM.

> Since vs. For

    Since = starting point in time

        I've lived here since 2010.

    For = duration of time

        I've lived here for 10 years.


### ðŸ“ Special Preposition Categories

> Phrasal Prepositions

Groups of words functioning as a single preposition

    Examples: in front of, because of, in spite of, on behalf of, next to

    Sentence: In spite of the rain, we had a picnic.


> Compound Prepositions

Two or more words combined

    Examples: into, onto, upon, within, without, throughout

    Sentence: She walked into the room.


> Participial Prepositions

Present participles functioning as prepositions

    Examples: regarding, concerning, considering, including

    Sentence: Regarding your question, here's my answer.


### ðŸ”— Prepositional Phrases


A preposition plus its object and any modifiers

    Structure: Preposition + Object (noun/pronoun) + Modifiers

    Functions:

        Adjectival: modifies a noun

            The house with the red door is mine.

        Adverbial: modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb

            She arrived after dinner.


## âš ï¸ Common Challenges and Mistakes

### 1. Ending Sentences with Prepositions

> Formal/old rule: Avoid ending sentences with prepositions

    Modern usage: Acceptable in informal English

    Examples:

        Formal: "About what are you thinking?"

        Informal: "What are you thinking about?"


### 2. Preposition Choices with Verbs/Adjectives

> Certain verbs and adjectives require specific prepositions:

|Verb/Adjective|	Correct Preposition|
|:---:|:---:|
|depend	|on (depend on)|
|afraid	|of (afraid of)|
|interested|	in (interested in)|
|good	|at (good at something)|
|married	|to (married to someone)|


### 3. Confusing Pairs

> To vs. Too vs. Two

    To: preposition (going to the store) or infinitive marker (to run)

    Too: adverb meaning "also" or "excessively"

    Two: number (2)

> Then vs. Than

    Then: adverb of time

    Than: preposition used in comparisons


## ðŸŒ Prepositions in Different Contexts

> Prepositions in Idioms

    Examples: at odds with, in the loop, on cloud nine, under the weather

    Sentence: She's feeling under the weather today.

> Prepositions with Gerunds

    Rule: Use prepositions before gerunds (-ing forms)

    Examples: insist on going, think about leaving, apologize for being late

> Prepositions in Questions

    Structure: Preposition + question word

    Examples: To whom did you speak? In which city do you live?

## ðŸ’¡ Tips for Mastering Prepositions

    Learn in Context: Memorize prepositions with example sentences

    Practice with Phrasal Verbs: Many phrasal verbs include prepositions (give up, look after)

    Use Visual Aids: Draw diagrams for prepositions of place

    Read Aloud: Practice prepositional phrases in full sentences

    Common Combinations: Study verb + preposition and adjective + preposition pairs


## ðŸŽ¯ Quick Reference Guide
Three Most Common Preposition Errors to Avoid

    Using "of" instead of "have" (could have, not could of)

    Confusing "in" and "on" for dates and times

    Omitting necessary prepositions (listen to music, not listen music)

When in Doubt, Remember:

    Time: Think clock (at), calendar (on), container (in)

    Place: Think address (at), surface (on), space (in)

    Movement: Think destination (to), origin (from), path (through)
