# Top "Did You Know?" Insights: English Basics

## Words & Spelling

> "I am" is the shortest complete sentence in English.

> The word "set" has the most definitions (over 430).

> "E" is the most common letter, appearing in about 11% of all words.

> "The quick brown fox jumps over a lazy dog" uses every letter of the alphabet (a pangram).


## Grammar & Structure

> The most common word is "the".

> "Go" is the shortest complete sentence in the imperative mood.

> "You" is both singular and plural (we lost "thou" for singular).


## History & Origins

> Modern English is often dated from William Shakespeare's time (~1500s-1600s).

> Many "basic" words (e.g., child, woman, water) are from Old English.

> A huge number of "advanced" words (e.g., science, government, art) came from French and Latin.


## Quirks

> "Queue" is still pronounced the same if you remove the last four letters.

> We drive on a parkway but park in a driveway.

> "Pronunciation" is often mispronounced.
