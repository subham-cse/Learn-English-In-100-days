Adverbs are versatile parts of speech that modify verbs, adjectives, other adverbs, or entire sentences. They provide additional information about how, when, where, why, or to what degree something happens.

## ğŸ” Definition

An adverb is a word that describes or gives more information about:

    A verb (She runs quickly)

    An adjective (The extremely tall building)

    Another adverb (He works very efficiently)

    A whole sentence (Fortunately, we arrived on time)


## ğŸ“Š Types of Adverbs

## 1. Adverbs of Manner (How?)

Describe how an action is performed

    Examples: quickly, slowly, carefully, loudly, well

    Sentence: She speaks confidently in public.


## 2. Adverbs of Time (When?)

Indicate when an action occurs

    Examples: now, then, yesterday, soon, already

    Sentence: We will leave tomorrow.


## 3. Adverbs of Place (Where?)

Show where an action happens

    Examples: here, there, everywhere, upstairs, outside

    Sentence: Please wait outside.


## 4. Adverbs of Frequency (How often?)

Express how frequently something happens

    Examples: always, often, sometimes, rarely, never

    Sentence: He usually exercises in the morning.


## 5. Adverbs of Degree (To what extent?)

Indicate the intensity or degree of an action, adjective, or another adverb

    Examples: very, too, quite, extremely, almost

    Sentence: It was extremely hot yesterday.


## 6. Adverbs of Certainty (How sure?)

Express the level of certainty about an action

    Examples: definitely, probably, certainly, maybe, perhaps

    Sentence: I will definitely attend the meeting.


## 7. Interrogative Adverbs (Question words)

Used to ask questions

    Examples: when, where, why, how

    Sentence: Where did you go yesterday?


## 8. Relative Adverbs

Introduce relative clauses

    Examples: when, where, why

    Sentence: This is the park where we first met.


## 9. Conjunctive Adverbs

Connect clauses or sentences

    Examples: however, therefore, moreover, nevertheless, meanwhile

    Sentence: It was raining; however, we still went hiking.

### General Rules
