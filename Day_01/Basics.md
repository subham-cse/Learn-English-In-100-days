## Overview
Welcome to **Day 1** of the "Learn English in 100 Days" course!  
Today, we start with the **foundations of English grammar**, focusing on **nouns** and **pronouns**. Understanding these basics is essential for constructing correct sentences and improving your communication skills.

## üìö Lesson Content

### 1. English Basics
- English is a **subject-verb-object language**, meaning most sentences follow the structure:  
  `Subject + Verb + Object`  
  Example: *She (S) reads (V) a book (O).*

- Parts of speech are the building blocks of grammar. Today, we cover **nouns** and **pronouns**.

---

### 2. Nouns
**Definition:** A noun is a word that **names a person, place, thing, or idea**.  

**Types of Nouns:**
1. **Common Noun** ‚Äì general name of a person, place, or thing  
   - Example: *teacher, city, book*
2. **Proper Noun** ‚Äì specific name, always capitalized  
   - Example: *Subham, Delhi, Titanic*
3. **Abstract Noun** ‚Äì names an idea, feeling, or quality  
   - Example: *happiness, courage, freedom*
4. **Collective Noun** ‚Äì names a group of people or things  
   - Example: *team, flock, family*
5. **Countable & Uncountable Noun**  
   - Countable: *apple, chair*  
   - Uncountable: *water, rice*

**Exercise:** Identify the nouns in the sentence:  
*"The teacher guided the students with patience."*

---

### 3. Pronouns
**Definition:** A pronoun **replaces a noun** to avoid repetition.  

**Common Types of Pronouns:**
1. **Personal Pronouns** ‚Äì I, you, he, she, it, we, they  
2. **Possessive Pronouns** ‚Äì mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs  
3. **Reflexive Pronouns** ‚Äì myself, yourself, himself, herself, themselves  
4. **Demonstrative Pronouns** ‚Äì this, that, these, those  
5. **Relative Pronouns** ‚Äì who, whom, which, that  

**Example Sentences:**  
- *She* is reading a book. (personal pronoun replacing a person)  
- This book is *mine*. (possessive pronoun)

**Exercise:** Replace the nouns with suitable pronouns:  
*"Subham and I went to the park. Subham and I played football."*

---

## üìå Key Takeaways
- Nouns **name things**, pronouns **replace nouns**.  
- Understanding nouns and pronouns helps **construct clear and concise sentences**.  
- Daily practice with examples improves **accuracy and confidence** in English.
